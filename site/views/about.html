About

My name is David Farrow. I am a passionate software developer and startup enthusiast. Im currently studying a Bachelor of Information and Communication Technology majoring in software development at Griffith University Gold Coast, and am expected to graduate in June 2016.

I love volunteering and getting involved in the IT communities. I recently attended Startup Weekend Gold Coast and Brisbane Creative Tech. where with a small team, in 54hrs, I cofounded justteachme.co and composit.co. I was able to volunteer at the YOW! Conference 2015, and the YOW! Lambda Jam 2016. And I have been a member of the ACS Gold Coast committee for the past year.

I am an ICT peer mentor and part-time software developer at Griffith university. I have recently been able to work on a internal project, where using the Myo armband I developed a desktop application that can monitor and display data for muscular injuries in the arm and shoulder. I am also a prominent member of the Startup and student user group Studio39.

Most recently I have been interning at Just Digital People for my Industry Affiliate Program. As they are in the process of bootstrapping a software development team I have had the amazing opportunity to be involved in the hiring process and take the role as team lead, managing 3 developers of various experience, with one working remotely from Melbourne.

In addition to my love of technology, I love listening to podcasts and a wide range of music from the 1960s to now (I own somewhere between 500 and 1000 vinyl records). I also try to stay active and love trying new things, like most rencently golf and basketball.
